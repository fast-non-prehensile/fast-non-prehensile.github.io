FAST: Friction-Aware non-Sliding Transport with Learned Motion-Based Non-Prehensile Constraints

Yuemin Mao1, Bardienus P. Duisterhof1, Moonyoung Lee1, Jeffrey Ichnowski1,
1Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University
Video

Abstract

Object transport tasks are fundamental in robotic automation, emphasizing the importance of efficient and secure methods for moving objects. Non-prehensile transport has the advantage of moving multiple objects simultaneously and handling objects that are difficult for rigid grasps, which can significantly improve transport efficiency. However, fast non-prehensile transport requires carefully-designed constraints to balance the unilateral forces on the objects to guarantee safety. We propose FAST: friction-aware non-sliding transport with learned motion-based non-prehensile constraints, aiming to transport objects on a tray at high speed with a robotic manipulator, preventing objects from falling or sliding. We first formulate a baseline Coulomb friction constraint with a measured static friction coefficient, which does not account for all forces in reality and leads to object sliding. We then propose learning a motion-based constraint to adjust the effective friction during optimization-based motion planning. To train a model for this constraint, we employ vibro-tactile sensing to identify the threshold velocity and acceleration that trigger sliding. We incorporate the motion-based constraint into an optimization-based motion planning framework to generate motions for a UR5e robot to transport various objects. Experimental results suggest the learned motion-based constraint reduces the displacement compared to the baseline Coulomb friction constraint up to 86.0%.
